GDPR Compliance Statement

Last Updated: June 4, 2025

1. Introduction

CountryMax Email Delivery is fully committed to complying with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (EU) 2016/679. This regulation enhances the protection of personal data of individuals within the European Union and affects how we handle such data in our email delivery services.

2. Data Controller and Processor

In the context of GDPR:

  • CountryMax Email Delivery acts as a Data Processor for the personal data contained in emails sent through our service.
  • Our customers act as Data Controllers for the personal data they send through our platform.

3. Lawful Basis for Processing

We ensure that all processing activities have a valid lawful basis under GDPR:

  • Consent: Where required, we ensure explicit consent is obtained and documented.
  • Contractual Necessity: Processing necessary for the performance of a contract.
  • Legitimate Interests: Processing based on legitimate interests, balanced against individual rights.

4. Data Subject Rights

We facilitate our customers in fulfilling data subject rights requests:

  • Right to Access: Individuals can request access to their personal data.
  • Right to Rectification: Individuals can request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Right to Erasure: Individuals can request deletion of their personal data ("right to be forgotten").
  • Right to Restriction: Individuals can request restriction of processing.
  • Right to Data Portability: Individuals can request their data in a structured format.
  • Right to Object: Individuals can object to certain types of processing.

5. Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

We offer a GDPR-compliant Data Processing Agreement to all customers handling EU resident data. Our DPA includes:

  • Clear roles and responsibilities of controller and processor
  • Security measures and confidentiality obligations
  • Procedures for data breach notification
  • Data transfer safeguards
  • Sub-processing requirements

To request our DPA, please contact us at privacy@countrymaxemail.com.

6. Data Transfers

When transferring personal data outside the EEA, we ensure adequate safeguards are in place:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
  • Adequacy decisions for recipient countries
  • Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs) where applicable

7. Data Protection Officer

We have appointed a Data Protection Officer (DPO) to oversee GDPR compliance:

8. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach, we will:

  • Notify affected customers without undue delay
  • Provide details of the breach and affected data
  • Assist in meeting GDPR notification requirements

9. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes outlined in our Privacy Policy, in accordance with GDPR's storage limitation principle.

10. Ongoing Compliance

We maintain GDPR compliance through:

  • Regular staff training on data protection
  • Annual GDPR audits and assessments
  • Privacy by Design and Default implementation
  • Maintaining Records of Processing Activities (ROPA)
